Localized Dreams—


In many areas of the United States, students are only allowed to attend schools within their district. The educational resources that are available to them are determined by the income of both the families and businesses in their localized area. This means that the quality of students’ education is based on the external factor of their family’s socioeconomic status instead of their own effort or potential.

Districting—


Most school districts in America are established based on township, city or other county-created boundaries. At every public school in America, a set amount of money is spent on each student per year. However, this amount is heavily dependent on a given area's local revenue, which leads lower-income districts to have much less funding for their public schools, and in turn, their students' education.
